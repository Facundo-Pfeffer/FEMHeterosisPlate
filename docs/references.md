# References

The following references support the formulation, implementation, meshing, and verification notes used in this repository.

[1] C. Geuzaine and J.-F. Remacle, "Gmsh: A three-dimensional finite element mesh generator with built-in pre- and post-processing facilities," *International Journal for Numerical Methods in Engineering*, vol. 79, no. 11, pp. 1309-1331, 2009.

[2] O. C. Zienkiewicz, R. L. Taylor, and S. Govindjee, *The Finite Element Method: Its Basis and Fundamentals*. Elsevier, 8th ed., Nov. 2024.

[3] S. Govindjee, "Lectures for CE 222: Finite Element Methods." Course lectures, University of California, Berkeley, Spring 2026 semester.

[4] L. Anand and S. Govindjee, *Continuum Mechanics of Solids*. Oxford University Press, July 2020.

[5] S. Govindjee, "Lectures for CE 231: Solid Mechanics." Course lectures, University of California, Berkeley, Fall 2025 semester.

[6] L. Anand and S. Govindjee, *Example Problems for Continuum Mechanics of Solids*. Independently published, 2020. Publication date: July 25, 2020.

---

## Where each reference is used

- **Gmsh reference**: optional Gmsh mesh-generation workflow and distance-field refinement strategy.
- **Zienkiewicz, Taylor, and Govindjee**: FE foundations, patch testing, mixed interpolation concerns, and plate/shell context.
- **CE 222 lectures**: weak forms, interpolation, assembly, quadrature, and verification framing.
- **Anand and Govindjee continuum mechanics references**: small-strain kinematics, constitutive modeling, stress resultants, and notation.
- **CE 231 lectures**: continuum and solid mechanics background behind plate reduction.
